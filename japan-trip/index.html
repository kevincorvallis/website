The Wakayama Hotfix

Four days, three onsen soaks, one itinerary now open for peer review.

I planned a four-day run down the Wakayama coast in October, and because I'm an engineer I built a webpage about it instead of just texting you. Everything below is real and mostly booked. This page has a comments section, which means the itinerary is now open source — tell me what I got wrong, what I'm missing, or what snack you want smuggled back. I will read all feedback from a bathtub facing the Pacific.

5 Sights

Engetsu Island

A rock arch famous for a sunset that lines up perfectly through the hole — at the equinox, which October is not. We'll be off by about three weeks and call it close enough.

Sunset itself is still worth it, roughly 5:30–6:00pm in October.

Shirasaki Marine Park

White limestone coast nicknamed "Japan's Aegean Sea," for the budget that didn't stretch to Santorini.

View on Google Maps →

Nachi Falls

Photo: Raita Futo, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Japan's tallest single-drop waterfall (133m), next to a red three-story pagoda. One photo, no notes.

Reached via the Daimonzaka stone-step trail through 800-year-old cedars — budget 2.5–3hrs round trip. No direct train from Shirahama/Kushimoto; JR to Kii-Katsuura then a local bus.

View on Google Maps →

Wakayama Castle

Every Japan itinerary is contractually required to include one castle. This is ours.

Built 1585 under Toyotomi Hideyoshi; blue-stone walls are a Kishu specialty.

View on Google Maps →

Sandanbeki Cliffs

50m sea cliffs with an elevator down into a smuggler's cave — the rare attraction with both drama and an accessible entrance.

View on Google Maps →

Food

Wakayama tuna sukiyaki, a hotel dinner buffet (free-flow drinks, once), and Wakayama ramen — a rich tonkotsu-shoyu bowl with a quirky help-yourself side table of boiled eggs and pressed mackerel sushi, honor system.

Practical Notes

October in Wakayama: pleasant mid-20s°C, some rain (~14 days/month, mostly light), typhoon risk much lower than Aug/Sept.

Onsen etiquette: wash thoroughly before entering, no swimsuits, small towel never touches the bathwater. Tattoo policy varies by hotel — worth confirming ahead since 2 of 3 nights are onsen resorts.

Open an issue

Known bugs: no equinox, possible rain. Feature requests, roasts, and souvenir orders all accepted — the itinerary ships in October regardless.
