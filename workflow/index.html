The Agent Graph

Seven agents, typed edges, and one rule: every report ends by naming who should look next.

I audited my AI tooling and cut 162 agent definitions down to the seven that had actually earned usage. What survived became a graph — each handoff is a typed edge, and every agent closes its report with a machine-readable next: line. Hover or tab through the nodes.

the gauntletHow designs earn the right to ship

Big changes — including this graph — go through an adversarial review pipeline before implementation: two different vendors' models attack the design blind, then attack each other's critiques, and the surviving findings get judged against the artifact.

0 · sealed takemy own findings, written down first so the panel can't anchor me
1 · blind takestwo models attack the design independently
2 · crossfireeach refutes or concedes the other's findings
3 · verdicthallucinations die, confirmed fixes fold into v2

This graph's own verdict: pass-with-fixes. One reviewer's only BLOCKER turned out to be hallucinated; the other's twelve findings included six that changed the design.
